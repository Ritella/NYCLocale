Knowing where to go in NYC is difficult.



Hi, my name is Rita Loiotile.
I was born and raised in Brooklyn.
Even as a native New Yorker, I never felt like I had enough knowledge of where to go in Queens.
For this reason, I avoided exploring it on my own.
NYCLocal(e) aims to help people overcome this decision paralysis by giving greater context to NYC outer boroughs.
The heat map reflects cultural hotspots.

I determined where New Yorkers spend their leisure time, by analyzing liquor licenses and subway turnstile data, both available at opendata.cityofnewyork.us.
These datasets gave me proxies of nightlife activity and weekend foot traffic, respectively.
Using these data, I performed an Independent Component Analysis to model leisure activity.
Finally, I validated my model with another dataset-- building construction permits.
Areas with a high amount of new construction predicted areas of increased weekend subway traffic.

Please explore the map and type in an address or intersection you're thinking of staying near.

You can find my analysis code on github and more information about me here.
I conceived of and executed this project in 3 weeks as an Insight NYC Data Science Fellow (Jan 2019).
